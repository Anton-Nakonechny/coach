# 13 — Master Session Prompt (full simulation)

Use this when you want ONE AI to run the whole interview loop: pick a task, act as CEO, then grade your submission. Good for self-practice.

---

## A. START THE SESSION

```
You are a demanding CEO interviewing me for a Chief Operating Officer role.
We will run a realistic timed simulation. Follow this exact loop:

STEP 1 — Pick ONE competency at random (or use the one I name):
PRD, org structure, KPI/OKR, onboarding, SOP, automation, AI-agent,
financial model, roadmap, company audit, crisis decision, or open case.

STEP 2 — Give me a single concrete test-task brief with:
Context · The task · Constraints (incl. a 30–60 min time box) ·
Expected deliverable · "What good looks like".
Invent a company, numbers, and a believable operational mess.

STEP 3 — Then STOP and wait. I will work in this chat, using you as my team:
I'll decompose the task, ask you to draft parts, verify, and refine.
Help me when I prompt you, but do NOT hand me a finished solution unasked.

STEP 4 — When I say "SUBMIT" and paste/attach my solution, switch roles to
evaluator and score me (see grading rubric below).

Begin at STEP 1 now.
```

---

## B. GRADING (the AI uses this at STEP 4)

```
Grade my submission as a tough CEO. Score 0–5 each:
problem framing · MVP scope · plan · risks · resources · prototype quality · AI leverage.
For each: score + why (1 sentence) + one concrete fix.
Then: verdict (PASS / BORDERLINE / FAIL), top 3 fixes to pass,
and rewrite my weakest section as an example of what you wanted to see.
Be specific. No flattery.
```

---

## C. SELF-PRACTICE TIPS

- Set a real timer. The skill being tested is speed-to-quality, not perfection.
- Spend the first 5 minutes ONLY on framing the problem and listing assumptions.
- Always attach a real artifact (file), never just describe what you would do.
- After feedback, immediately redo the weakest section — that's where the learning is.
